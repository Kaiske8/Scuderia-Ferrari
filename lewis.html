Lewis Hamilton

Lewis Hamilton is a British racing driver who has won seven Formula 1 World Championships, tying the record held by Michael Schumacher. Known for his aggressive driving style and exceptional skill on the track, Hamilton has become one of the most successful and influential figures in motorsport history.

Biography

Born on 7 January 1985 in Stevenage, England, Lewis Hamilton discovered karting at age eight. His father Anthony worked multiple jobs to fund his early career, and a young Lewis famously approached McLaren boss Ron Dennis at an awards ceremony aged ten, telling him he wanted to race for the team one day. Dennis took note — and seven years later, made it happen.

Hamilton's arrival in Formula 1 in 2007 was seismic. He came within a single point of the championship as a rookie, then won it the following year in dramatic fashion on the final corner of the final lap in Brazil.

In 2025 he made the most surprising move of his career — leaving Mercedes to join Scuderia Ferrari in a chapter many called the most romantic signing in F1 history.

Did You Know?

Hamilton has won more Formula 1 races than any driver in history, surpassing Michael Schumacher's record of 91 wins in 2020.

He was knighted by King Charles III in 2021, becoming Sir Lewis Hamilton.

Outside racing he is a fashion designer, animal rights activist, and co-owner of the Denver Broncos NFL team.
